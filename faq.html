Mission History

Each of these rovers is more than machinery — they’re markers of time, technology, and truth-seeking. Here's a clear breakdown of when they landed and what changed because of them.

  • 1997 – Sojourner: First mobile rover on Mars. Showed we could drive remotely with delay and minimal power.
  • 2004 – Spirit & Opportunity: Meant for 90 days, but lasted years. They sent back images, data, and evidence of ancient water.
  • 2012 – Curiosity: A lab on wheels. Analyzed rock layers and detected organics. Confirmed Mars could have once supported life.
  • 2021 – Perseverance: The boldest yet. Deploys drones, produces oxygen, and sets the stage for human missions.
