Frequently Asked Questions

Why does NASA keep sending rovers to Mars?
Mars is the most Earth-like planet within our reach. Each rover deepens our understanding of planetary formation, water history, and biological potential.
How are rovers controlled from Earth?
Rovers can't be controlled in real time. Commands are sent in packets, and the rovers follow instructions using autonomous navigation when needed.
Have any rovers ever found life?
None have found living organisms, but they’ve discovered organic molecules and ancient lakebeds that suggest Mars was once habitable.
What powers the rovers?
Early rovers used solar panels, while Curiosity and Perseverance use nuclear power systems to operate continuously through dust storms and cold nights.
What happens when a rover shuts down?
The mission is archived. Engineers analyze its final data and performance logs. Each end-of-mission teaches NASA how to build the next generation better.
