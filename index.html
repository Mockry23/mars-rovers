Welcome

I’m a political science and cybersecurity student. My curiosity about how systems work doesn't stop at Earth. This site explores NASA's Mars Rovers — machines designed to operate in isolation, analyze data, and relay truth from another world. That mission resonates with me.

Featured Rovers

Curiosity

Launched in 2012, Curiosity confirmed Mars once had the elements needed for life. It became a self-sustaining mobile lab, showing us that science doesn’t sleep — even 140 million miles away.

Perseverance

More than a rover — it’s a prelude to human colonization. Deployed in 2021, Perseverance is not just collecting Martian rock samples, it’s testing oxygen production for future crews.

Spirit

Spirit was the rover that never gave up. It lasted far beyond its expected mission and sent back some of the most iconic images from Mars.

Opportunity

Opportunity was an overachiever — built to last 90 days, but survived for almost 15 years. It traveled more than 28 miles and sent home over 200,000 images.

Sojourner

Sojourner was the pioneer. In 1997, it became the first mobile robot to operate on another planet, proving the viability of robotic surface exploration.
