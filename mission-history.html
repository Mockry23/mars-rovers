Mission History

I don’t just look at rovers as machines — I look at them as milestones. Each one was a shift in what we thought was possible. Below is a breakdown of when key players landed on Mars and what made them unforgettable.

  • 1997 – Sojourner: The first mobile rover to touch down on another planet. Small, slow, but historic. It proved remote exploration was feasible.
  • 2004 – Spirit & Opportunity: Twin rovers designed for 90 days. Spirit lasted 6 years, Opportunity lasted nearly 15. They redefined endurance.
  • 2012 – Curiosity: A fully equipped science lab on wheels. It confirmed ancient Mars had conditions suitable for life.
  • 2021 – Perseverance: Not just researching, but preparing the planet for future human arrival. It dropped a helicopter, generated oxygen, and is caching samples for Earth return.
